Hillier College of Architecture and Design

The Hillier College of Architecture and Design, commonly known as HCAD, is home to many of students within the architecture, industrial design, interior design, etc. majors. HCAD emphasizes a multidisciplinary approach, integrating design with technology to address real-world challenges. Its programs are accredited and highly regarded, particularly the Bachelor of Architecture (B.Arch.), which is accredited by the National Architectural Accrediting Board (NAAB). The curriculum combines studio-based learning, technical courses, and liberal arts education, ensuring students are well-rounded and prepared for diverse professional opportunities.

Facilities at HCAD include state-of-the-art studios, computer labs, and fabrication workshops equipped with the latest technology, such as 3D printers, laser cutters, and virtual reality tools. These resources allow students to experiment with innovative design methods and build prototypes, fostering hands-on learning experiences.

The faculty members of HCAD are professionals from the industry and researchers with practical knowledge and academic stringency in teaching. Many collaborate on projects with students that investigate the issues of sustainable design, urban planning, and state-of-the-art technologies, reflecting the college's commitment to solving world and community-based challenges.
